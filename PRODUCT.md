# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary user: the developer/owner themselves — a single person at BOL tracking their own projects and KPI goals. Context: opened at a desk during a work session to check progress, add tasks, or log updates. Not a multi-user reporting tool; a personal productivity layer on top of Google Sheets.

## Product Purpose

A personal KPI and project-progress dashboard. Lets the user create projects with status and priority, break them into tasks, track completion percentages, and sync everything to a Google Sheet for persistence. Success: at any moment, the user can open the dashboard and immediately know what is in progress, what is done, and what still needs attention.

## Brand Personality

Warm, unhurried, clear. Think of it as a well-designed personal notebook — not a startup SaaS product, not an enterprise system. Three words: **calm, legible, personal**.

Reference: Notion — specifically the spacious layout, document-like feel, content-first hierarchy, and the sense that the UI is there to serve the content rather than shout about itself.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS-y / startup-cool** — no gradient hero text, glassmorphism cards, neon on dark, or trendy accent combos.
- **Enterprise heavy** — no dense blue-gray tables, overloaded navbars, or the SAP/Oracle feel.
- **Spreadsheet-like** — not a grid of tiny text; data should breathe.

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the hero** — the dashboard, data, and status carry the interface; chrome recedes.
2. **Warmth through space, not decoration** — generous whitespace and comfortable type sizes convey approachability; no illustrations or playful shapes needed.
3. **Clarity at first glance** — status and progress are visually unambiguous without digging into detail.
4. **Honest feedback** — actions, errors, and sync state communicate clearly and without alarm.
5. **No surprises** — interactions behave predictably; nothing clever for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG level required. Good baseline: readable contrast, legible type sizes, focus states on interactive elements, and sensible tab order. Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations added.
